Sentences


Today’s key takeaways

There are four types of sentences that we use every day. Each one has a different job and helps us communicate in a special way. Let’s review them with some easy rules and examples!

Declarative Sentences

  • Rule: These sentences tell us something or give information.
  • They end with a full stop (.).
  • Example: “The sun is shining.”
  • Declarative sentences are like telling a story or stating a fact

Interrogative Sentences

  • Rule: These sentences ask a question.
  • They end with a question mark (?).
  • Example: “What is your name?”
  • Interrogative sentences are asking for information or trying to find out something.

Imperative Sentences

  • Rule: These sentences give a command or make a request.
  • They usually end with a full stop (.), but can also end with an exclamation mark (!) if the command is strong.
  • Example: “Please close the door.”
  • Imperative sentences tell someone to do something.

Exclamatory Sentences

  • Rule: These sentences show strong feelings or emotions.
  • They always end with an exclamation mark (!).
  • Example: “Wow, that’s amazing!”
  • Exclamatory sentences are used when we are excited, surprised, or really happy.

  • Declarative: Tells us something. (The sky is blue.)
  • Interrogative: Asks a question. (Is it raining?)
  • Imperative: Gives a command or request. (Sit down, please.)
  • Exclamatory: Shows strong feelings. (That’s fantastic!)
